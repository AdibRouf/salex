Autonomous Vehicle Decision Model

Daniel Baker • Adib Rouf • Guo Zhefan

Overview

Our Autonomous Vehicle Decision Model represents cutting-edge research in AI-driven transportation. This project combines computer vision, machine learning, and decision theory to create intelligent systems capable of making real-time driving decisions. Through comprehensive video analysis and heatmap visualization, we demonstrate how autonomous vehicles can perceive, process, and respond to complex traffic scenarios with human-like intelligence and enhanced safety protocols.

Video Analysis

What to Expect: After processing through our model, the input video will be analyzed frame by frame to identify vehicles, pedestrians, traffic signals, and road conditions. The system will highlight critical decision points, predict potential hazards, and demonstrate the reasoning process behind each autonomous driving decision. You'll see real-time confidence scores and alternative action paths that the AI considers.

Attention Heatmap
Heatmap Insights: The heatmap visualization reveals where our autonomous vehicle model focuses its attention during decision-making. Warmer colors (red/yellow) indicate areas of high importance, while cooler colors (blue/green) show regions of lower priority. This attention mechanism helps us understand how the AI prioritizes different elements in the driving environment - from oncoming traffic and pedestrians to traffic signs and road boundaries.
